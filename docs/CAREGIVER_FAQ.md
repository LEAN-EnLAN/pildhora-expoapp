## General Questions

### What is PILDHORA?

PILDHORA is a comprehensive medication management system that helps caregivers monitor and manage medications for their patients. It consists of a physical device that dispenses medications and a mobile app for remote management.

### Who is PILDHORA for?

PILDHORA is designed for:
- Professional caregivers managing multiple patients
- Family members caring for elderly relatives
- Healthcare facilities managing patient medications
- Anyone responsible for medication adherence

### What devices are supported?

**Mobile Devices**:
- iOS 13.0 or later (iPhone, iPad)
- Android 8.0 or later

**PILDHORA Hardware**:
- All PILDHORA medication dispensers
- WiFi-enabled models
- Bluetooth-enabled models (coming soon)

- Phone: 1-800-PILDHORA
- Website: pildhora.com/pricing

Pricing varies based on:
- Number of patients
- Features required

## Account and Access

### How do I create a caregiver account?

1. Download the PILDHORA app
2. Tap "Sign Up"
3. Enter your email and password
4. Select "Caregiver" as your role
5. Complete your profile
6. Verify your email

### Can I have both patient and caregiver accounts?

No, each account can only have one role. If you need both:
- Create separate accounts with different emails
- Use one account for caregiving
- Use another for personal medication management

### How do I change my password?

1. Go to Settings
2. Tap "Security"
3. Tap "Change Password"
4. Enter current password
5. Enter new password
6. Confirm new password
7. Tap "Save"

### What if I forget my password?

1. On login screen, tap "Forgot Password"
2. Enter your email address
3. Check email for reset link
4. Click link and create new password
5. Log in with new password

### Can I use the same account on multiple devices?

Yes! You can log in to your account on:
- Multiple phones
- Tablets
- Different devices simultaneously

Your data syncs across all devices automatically.

## Device Management

### How do I link a device?

1. Get the device ID from the patient or device label
2. Tap "Device" in Quick Actions
3. Tap "Link New Device"
4. Enter the device ID
5. Tap "Link Device"

### What is a device ID?

A device ID is a unique identifier for each PILDHORA device. It's:
- Printed on the device label
- Minimum 5 characters
- Format: Letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores
- Example: "DEVICE-001" or "PILDHORA-ABC123"

### Can multiple caregivers link to the same device?

Yes! Multiple caregivers can link to the same device and manage the same patient. This enables:
- Shared caregiving responsibilities
- 24/7 coverage
- Coordinated care
- Backup caregivers

### How do I unlink a device?

1. Go to Device Management
2. Find the device in your list
3. Tap "Unlink"
4. Confirm the action

Note: This removes your access but doesn't affect the patient or device.

### What does "device offline" mean?

Device offline means:
- Device is not connected to the internet
- Last seen timestamp is shown
- Medication alarms still work locally
- Configuration changes will sync when online

### How do I troubleshoot an offline device?

1. Check device power
2. Verify WiFi connection
3. Ensure device is in WiFi range
4. Restart the device
5. Check router is working

### Can I configure device settings remotely?

Yes! You can configure:
- Alarm mode (sound, vibrate, both, silent)
- Alarm volume
- LED intensity
- LED color

Changes sync automatically when device is online.

## Patient Management

### How many patients can I manage?

There's no limit! You can manage as many patients as needed.

### How do I switch between patients?

Use the Patient Selector at the top of the dashboard:
1. Swipe left/right to see all patients
2. Tap a patient card to switch
3. Dashboard updates to show that patient's data

### Can I see all patients at once?

No, the dashboard shows one patient at a time. To view multiple patients:
- Switch between them using Patient Selector
- Generate reports for each patient
- Use the Events Registry with patient filter

### What information can I see about patients?

You can see:
- Patient name
- Device status (online/offline)
- Battery level
- Medication list
- Medication events
- Adherence statistics
- Last medication taken

### Can I add patient notes?

Currently, use the Tasks feature for patient-specific notes. A dedicated notes feature is coming soon.

### How do I remove a patient from my list?

Unlink the patient's device:
1. Go to Device Management
2. Find the device
3. Tap "Unlink"
4. Confirm

This removes your access but doesn't delete patient data.

## Medication Management

### How do I add a medication?

1. Select the patient
2. Tap "Medications" in Quick Actions
3. Tap the "+" button
4. Follow the wizard:
   - Choose icon and name
   - Set dosage
   - Create schedule
   - Configure inventory (optional)
5. Tap "Save"

### Can I edit a medication after adding it?

Yes! 
1. Go to Medications
2. Tap the medication card
3. Tap "Edit"
4. Make changes
5. Tap "Save"

All changes are tracked in the Events Registry.

### How do I delete a medication?

1. Go to Medications
2. Tap the medication card
3. Tap "Delete"
4. Confirm deletion

Note: Deletion is permanent and creates an event in the registry.

### What is inventory tracking?

Inventory tracking helps you:
- Monitor medication quantities
- Get low quantity alerts
- Plan refills in advance
- Track usage patterns

Enable it when adding or editing a medication.

### How do I update medication quantity?

1. Tap the medication card
2. Tap "Update Inventory"
3. Enter new quantity
4. Tap "Save"

### Can I set up refill reminders?

Yes! When inventory tracking is enabled:
1. Set low quantity threshold
2. You'll get alerts when quantity is low
3. Plan refills accordingly

### How many times per day can I schedule a medication?

No limit! Add as many times as needed:
- Once daily
- Twice daily
- Three times daily
- Or more

### Can I schedule medications for specific days?

Currently, medications can be:
- Daily (every day)
- Weekly (specific days)
- As needed (no schedule)

Custom schedules are coming soon.

## Events and Reporting

### What are medication events?

Events are records of medication-related activities:
- Medication created
- Medication updated
- Medication deleted
- Dose taken
- Dose missed

### How do I view events?

1. Tap "Events Registry" in Quick Actions
2. See chronological list of all events
3. Tap an event for details

### Can I filter events?

Yes! Filter by:
- Date range
- Event type
- Medication name
- Patient (if managing multiple)

### How do I generate a report?

1. Go to Events Registry
2. Apply filters if desired
3. Tap menu icon (⋮)
4. Select "Generate Report"
5. Choose format (PDF or CSV)
6. Share or save

### What formats are available for reports?

- **PDF**: For printing or sharing with doctors
- **CSV**: For importing into spreadsheets

### Can I schedule automatic reports?

Not currently, but this feature is planned. For now:
- Generate reports manually
- Set a task reminder to generate reports
- Export data regularly

### How long are events stored?

Events are stored indefinitely unless you delete them. You can:
- View historical events
- Generate reports for any date range
- Export data for backup

## Technical Questions

### What internet connection is required?

**For Mobile App**:
- WiFi or cellular data
- Stable connection recommended
- Works on 3G, 4G, 5G, WiFi

**For Device**:
- WiFi connection required
- 2.4GHz or 5GHz networks supported
- Minimum 1 Mbps speed

### Does the app work offline?

Partially:
- ✓ View cached data
- ✓ See recent events
- ✗ Cannot make changes
- ✗ Cannot sync events
- ✗ Real-time updates disabled

### How much data does the app use?

Very little:
- Average: 5-10 MB per month
- Mostly for syncing events
- Real-time updates use minimal data
- Reports and images use more

### Is the app available in other languages?

Currently available in:
- English
- Spanish (Español)

More languages coming soon!

### What are the system requirements?

**iOS**:
- iOS 13.0 or later
- iPhone 6s or newer
- iPad (5th generation) or newer
- 100 MB free storage

**Android**:
- Android 8.0 or later
- 2 GB RAM minimum
- 100 MB free storage

### Can I use the app on a tablet?

Yes! The app works on:
- iPads
- Android tablets
- Optimized for larger screens

## Privacy and Security

### Is my data secure?

Yes! We use:

Yes! PILDHORA is HIPAA compliant and follows all healthcare data protection regulations.

- Patient data remains intact
- Other caregivers retain access
- Events you created remain in registry
